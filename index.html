About

Hadi Reisizadeh

Postdoctoral Associate · Optimization for AI Lab · University of Minnesota

I am a Postdoctoral Associate at the Optimization for AI Lab, University of Minnesota. I received my Ph.D. and M.Sc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering (with a minor in Computer Science) from the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, and my B.Sc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Isfahan University of Technology.

My research focuses on LLM safety, optimization, and trustworthy AI. I have discovered that state-of-the-art unlearning methods fail to erase knowledge under probabilistic decoding, and developed BLUR, a bi-level optimization framework achieving up to 19% higher unlearning efficiency on LLaMA-2-7B and Zephyr-7B-Beta. I also proposed an edge-preserving chain-of-thought condensation method delivering lossless reasoning accuracy at 34% lower training cost across Qwen and LLaMA families.

I seek to advance AI systems that are not only capable, but whose behavior can be verified, corrected, and trusted.
